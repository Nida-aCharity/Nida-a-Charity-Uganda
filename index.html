üïå Zakat

Zakat is one of the Five Pillars of Islam and an obligation for every Muslim who meets the necessary criteria of wealth. It is a means of purifying your income and wealth by giving a portion to those in need.

‚ÄúTake from their wealth a charity by which you purify them and cause them increase...‚Äù
‚Äî Surah At-Tawbah (9:103)

Who Should Pay Zakat?

  • Muslims whose wealth exceeds the Nisab (minimum amount)
  • Held for one full lunar year
  • Applies to savings, gold, silver, business income, investments, and more

Zakat Rate

You are required to pay 2.5% of your wealth if it exceeds the Nisab.

Zakat Nisab (Minimum Threshold)

As of now, the minimum amount (Nisab) required for a Muslim to be eligible to pay Zakat is based on either gold or silver:

  • Gold Nisab(85g): $9,396.75
  • Silver Nisab(595g): $630.70

üìå Most scholars recommend using the Silver Nisab for broader Zakat eligibility and greater charity impact.
